Anjelica Huston, born July 8, 1951, is an American actress, author, director, producer, and former fashion model whose career spans over six decades. She came from Hollywood stock, as the daughter of director John Huston and granddaughter of actor Walter Huston.

Her first role was in her father’s ‘A Walk with Love and Death’ (1969), but her breakout role wouldn’t be until their collab on ‘Prizzi’s Honor’ (1985). She would win an Oscar for the role. From there, there would be Oscar-nominated roles in ‘Enemies, A Love Story’ (1989) and ‘The Grifters’ (1990),

BAFTA noms would come for her two Woody Allen collabs, ‘Crimes and Misdemeanors’ (1989) and ‘Manhattan Murder Mystery’ (1993). Anjelica was recognized by the Golden Globes for playing as Morticia Addams in ‘The Addams Family’ (1991) and its sequel ‘Addams Family Values’ (1993). Other acclaimed roles include ‘The Witches’ (1990), Wes Anderson’s ‘The Royal Tenenbaums’ (2001), ‘The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou’ (2004), and ‘The Darjeeling Limited’ (2007), the mockumentary ‘This Is Spinal Tap’ (1984), ‘The Dead’ (1987), ‘Ever After’ (1998), ‘Buffalo ’66’ (1998), ‘Daddy Day Care’ (2003), ’50/50′ (2011), and ‘John Wick: Chapter 3’.
