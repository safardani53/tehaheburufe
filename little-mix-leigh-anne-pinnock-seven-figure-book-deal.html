Little Mixs Leigh-Anne Pinnock in seven-figure book deal to tell her story of life in pop and sol

August 27, 2024
blog

LEIGH-ANNE PINNOCK is having her say, weeks away from stepping out of one of the biggest girl groups of all time.

After more than ten years in Little Mix I can reveal that as well as forging her solo career, a wide range of projects include a major debut book.

After the huge success of her acclaimed BBC Three documentary Race, Pop And Power, the singer is now negotiating a book deal with several major publishing houses for the autobiographical insight, which will focus on identity and her own experiences at the heart of British popular culture.

A source tells me: “This is stuff she’s passionate about - it isn’t going to be an expose on life in a pop band, more an insight into her feelings about who she has become in that time, what she stands for and what she believes in.

"The reaction to her documentary really spurred Leigh-Anne to want to do more. She knows her platform can help other people too and she wants to represent as many young women like her as she can.

“This is about stepping out from a band and defining who she is going to be as an independent woman and as an artist going forwards. She’s very excited at the prospect of working on it and getting it out to fans.”

Little Mix will shortly head out on the road for their final run of tour dates as a band — at least for now — and are expected to go out with a bang in their final performances of more than a decade of hits which has seen them become the most successful girl group of their generation.

Each of the remaining three members are in the process of mapping out their future career plans, combining solo music with film and TV
projects, fashion and other ideas.

Former bandmate Jesy Nelson has already begun releasing music alone, while pals Perrie Edwards and Jade Thirwall are currently negotiating their own new contracts to develop as solo artists.

A pal adds: “Leigh-Anne has a pretty clear vision of where she wants things to go after the band. She’s signed with a very established and successful management company, got a great record deal, appeared in the movie Boxing Day, alongside an all-black cast, and co-hosted the Mobo Awards — all just a few months after becoming a mum for the first time.

“She’s loved being in the band and has great memories of their experiences, but she’s definitely ready to step out on her own.”

With the right team behind her and a lorry-load of drive and ambition, you wouldn’t bet against her killing it alone.

Kanye's royally messed up now

KANYE West has made some questionable decisions in recent weeks but hiring the so-called journalist who last month claimed The Queen had died is one of the strangest.

Jason Lee runs US blog website Hollywood Unlocked.

In February he claimed to have the exclusive news about the Queen passing away, then doubled down on the story.

But clearly Kanye – who calls himself YE – isn’t put off by his dodgy reporting, as Jason is now on the rapper-cum-designer’s payroll as his media man ager.

A music insider said: “Kanye and Jason have known each other for a while but spent more time together at the beginning of the year.

"Now Jason is working for him in the role of a media manager, helping broker deals and he is in charge with his public image.

“From the outside it seems totally strange to take him on, especially after what happened with the Queen.

“But he wants to be surrounded by people who know how the media game works and reckons he shares a vision with Jason.”

I had been wondering why Kanye was getting such positive publicity on Jason’s website recently, now that’s all explained.

The Jesus Walks musician could use some help though.

His latest album Donda 2 hasn’t sold enough copies to made a mark on the charts after it was released exclusively via the Stem Player – his own product for playing music.

Stunts like that may make a fortune for Kanye by persuading some of his fans to buy the device, but in the long run it’ll just mean people switch off altogether.
