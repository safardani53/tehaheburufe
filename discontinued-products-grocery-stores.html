Four discontinued products you'll never see in grocery stores again - and fans are furious

August 21, 2024
blog

SHOPPERS may be disappointed to hear that some of their favorite products have disappeared from shelves forever.

In the past year, several major brands have made changes or ditched some much-loved grocery items.

Here are some of the items that have been taken off the shelves that you might want to make a note of.

1. SNACKWELL'S COOKIES

For two decades, Snackwell's made fat-free cookies in a variety of flavors including devil's food cake, creme and shortbread.

But in 2022, the brand announced its retirement.

A statement on its website said: "After thirty years of providing your favorite snacks, the Snackwell’s brand has been retired.

Fans have taken to social media to reminisce about the beloved brand.

While another tweeted: "I just discovered #Snackwells are discontinued, and I am so angry.

2. CHOCO TACO

People everywhere mourned the loss of the popular Choco Taco.

The fan-favorite dessert was a hit among foodies, but Klondike decided to ax the ice cream product anyway.

On July 25, 2022, Klondike announced its final decision regarding the fate of its longstanding ice cream product, the Choco Taco.

On Twitter, the dessert company revealed: "A necessary but unfortunate part of this process is that we sometimes must discontinue products, even a beloved item like Choco Taco.

"We know this may be very disappointing, and hope you’ll try our other delicious frozen treats!"

After almost 40 years since its conception, consumers would no longer be able to purchase Choco Taco.

On Twitter, one upset fan said: "At some point, you ate the last choco taco you’d ever eat and didn’t even know it."

While a second consumer tweeted: "Going to challenge Biden in the primary based solely on his inadequate response to the choco taco cancellation.

"America needs a president who can put our chocolate needs first."

3. KROGER'S FIZZ & CO. SELTZERS

Kroger officially confirmed to its customers in October last year that the Fizz & Co. Seltzers had been permanently discontinued.

Fans of the drink have been calling for its return, even creating a Facebook group to rally behind getting their favorite flavors back.

Some users shared emails they wrote to corporate, asking for the return of the drink.

“We know that customers enjoy our Kroger Brand products and appreciate your support of our private label brands,” read a message from the Kroger executive office.

“In reaching out to our Corporate Food Technology group, it was confirmed this product was discontinued due to low volume sales in all of our Kroger divisions.”

4. HONEST TEA

Coca-Cola announced last year that Honest Tea would no longer be part of its catalog.

The company announced that Gold Peak would be its main "ready-to-drink" tea.

But fans on social media are still unhappy about the decision.

One Twitter user wrote: "The way gold peak can’t even come close to honest tea."

And a second added: "I must have somehow missed Honest Tea being discontinued. Was the best tea brand."

Honest Tea was founded in 1998 by Seth Goldman and Barry Nalebuff.

Coca-Cola became a stakeholder in 2008 before fully acquiring the brand in 2011.

Although, Honest Kids is still available on grocery shelves.
