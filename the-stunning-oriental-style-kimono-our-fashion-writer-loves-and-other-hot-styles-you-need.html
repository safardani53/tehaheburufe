I WAS lucky enough to go on an incredible trip to China earlier this month, and even though the sun has been shining in Blighty, I’m still suffering from some serious holiday blues.

While I managed to wear my H&M Anna Glover blossom print dress in Shanghai, I’m currently racking up a huge credit card bill in an attempt to lift my spirits after the holiday of a lifetime.

Thankfully, the high street has got my back as all of my favourite stores are full of Oriental-inspired buys.

It is seriously helping me cling on to my travels across the Far East.

With delicate blossom prints, luxurious silky textures and shapes that will seriously flatter anyone’s curves (I should know, I have plenty!) a little Eastern piece will make a cheap and cheerful outfit look way more expensive.
So even if you’re not heading to Asia any time soon, or even anywhere out of the UK grab yourself one of these bargain buys.

CELEB STYLE CRUSH: BLAKE LIVELY 

May has been a big month for comic book fans like myself. But while most people are watching Ryan Reynolds in Deadpool, I’m currently watching his wife, Blake Lively’s wardrobe instead.

Now it’s no surprise that Blake is a style icon, after having one of the most enviable TV wardrobes whilst playing Serena in Gossip Girl, which is still one of my favourite TV shows ever in case you’re wondering.  

Earlier this week she supported Ryan Reynolds (seriously, these two are the definition of #couplegoals) at the Deadpool premier wearing a black and red outfit inspired by the movie. In my eyes, anyone who manages to make a superhero’s logo look this good is a hero in my eyes!

My main reasons for my serious style crush include the fact that Blake is one of those rare celebs who doesn’t employ a stylist. Unlike the likes of Holly Willoughby, this A-lister literally picks out her outfits all by herself.

Secondly, she knows how to dress for her curves. Like last weekend when she wore a pretty pastel dress by Jenny Packham which showed off her natural looking hourglass figure – something that seems to be pretty unusual in a world run by models and Kardashians.

Plus, she’ll be the first to admit that sometimes you have to look like you don’t love your friends as she posted a snap joking that giving them a huge would seriously ruin her Chloe and Gucci outfit.

And I know the Met Gala was so last week, but I can’t talk about Blake Lively without taking a moment to think about that INCREDIBLE Atelier Versace dress, which took over 600 hours to make! Now imagine being able to step out in that.

In short, I wish I was Blake Lively. Or at least access to her wardrobe.
