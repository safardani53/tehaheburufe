MANY a good tune is played on a STRADIVARIUS.

IN today’s Mansionbet Yorkshire Cup I’m expecting a virtuoso performance from John Gosden’s star stayer.

Wins in the Queen’s Vase and Goodwood Cup last season shows he is a horse with the potential to be top class.

2.20 YORK: Add a few Strings to your punting bow.

STRINGS OF LIFE looked all speed when making a winning debut at Newmarket last month.

Subsequent Salisbury winner Cotubanama was back in third so the form has some substance and today’s track should be ideal.

There’s plenty of credible rivals in an above-average renewal of this Listed contest. Carrie’s Vision hit the target at the first time of asking at Yarmouth. She looks smart.

On The Stage, Nicki’s Angel and last week’s Chester winner No Lippy are others in with a shout.

2.35 NEWBURY: I’m going for the Power to have the glory.

DARK POWER won a brace of handicaps last season before running below-par on his final two starts.

There were excuses for those efforts and he looks like a horse sure to improve a good deal for being gelded over the winter. He’s yet to race on fast ground and he’s certainly bred for it.

Baron Bolt ran a belter when a neck second to smart sprinter Gifted Master at Newmarket on 1000 Guineas day. He should go well again but is not easy to win with.

I’m more worried about Ultimate Avenue. He was runner-up in a hot handicap for three-year-olds at Newmarket’s July Meeting last season and he’s had a breathing operation over the winter.

2.55 YORK: AWESOMETANK should put some cash in the bank.

She won a nursery over today’s course and distance last season before finishing fourth in a Newmarket Listed contest behind smart prospect Hadith.

I’m confident she will have improved enough over the winter to take this prize. Threading won the Group 2 Lowther Stakes at this track last summer before disappointing in the Cheveley Park.

She again ran below expectations in the Nell Gwyn at Newmarket and she is banking on the drop in class and return to York doing the trick.

Sheikha Reika – off the mark at Newmarket last month – could easily be up to this level. She’s my idea of the main danger.

3.30 YORK: It’s worth fiddling around with STRADIVARIUS.

I reckon he’s going to be the staying star of the summer and I fancy him to kick off the season with victory in the Yorkshire Cup.

He followed his Queen’s Vase win at Royal Ascot by seeing off the older stayers in the Goodwood Cup before going close when third in the St Leger.

His record on ground without soft in the description is three wins from four starts and he looks very solid today.

Willie Mullins’ pair Max Dynamite – third in the Melbourne Cup – and Wicklow Brave both went to Cheltenham and Punchestown.

I just prefer Wicklow Brave but Call To Mind could easily be the one to chase home Stradivarius.

4.05 YORK: Chisel out some profit on MASTER CARPENTER.

Rod Millman’s hope won this race by five lengths 12 months ago. That was on soft ground but he’s got plenty of form on a faster surface and he’s lower in the weights this time.

He used Newbury’s Spring Cup as a prep race last year and he’s followed the path.

Valcartier chased home smart prospect Leader’s Legacy on his first run for 10 months at Wolverhampton. He likes fast ground and looks an obvious player.

Capton has claims if ready to go on his return and Thundering Blue is another to consider.
