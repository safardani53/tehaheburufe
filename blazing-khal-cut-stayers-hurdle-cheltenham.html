IS this the cut that could ruin a Cheltenham Festival dream?

This amazing close-up photo shows the nasty wound Stayers' Hurdle favourite Blazing Khal picked up in his most recent win.

Back with a bang!

Blazing Khal defies a 428-day lay-off by coming from off the pace to land the feature Grade 2 William Hill Boyne Hurdle in some style under a confident Philip Byrnes.

Charles Byrnes' stable star wiped the floor with rivals when returning from 428 days off to land the Grade 2 Boyne Hurdle at Navan on February 12.

But this image from February 24, taken when the seven-year-old enjoyed a day on Beale Beach in County Kerry, Ireland, reveals just why the horse could now miss the Festival.

Canny trainer Byrnes says the horse must make it through a do-or-die fitness test on Friday.

Blazing Khal is currently 3-1 fav for the Thursday showpiece, with Teahupoo a close second-fav at 4-1.

The former has been on antibiotics since picking up his lesion - and is in a race against time to be fit.
