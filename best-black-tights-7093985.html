The 10 Best Black Tights of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

August 23, 2024
blog

Ah, the quest for the perfect black tight. 

Whether you’re looking for shapewear-inspired tights or a pair of fleece-lined ones that’ll leave you feeling cozy and toasty for those cold months, there’s a black tight for every occasion, and you can't have too many pairs. “Think of them like socks,” says NYC-based personal stylist Adrian Causing “With the biggest trend of 2023 being ‘revenge dressing’ you’ll want a whole slew of them in your arsenal.” 

We tested 28 pairs of black tights from celeb-loved brands to Amazon’s best-kept secrets, evaluating each one for its rip-resistance, comfort, how they hold up in the wash, and more. After the testing period was complete, we ultimately landed on these picks whose quality we stand by.

Keep reading to learn more about the best black tights that PEOPLE Tested.

Things to Consider Before Buying Black Tights

  • Denier: Before purchasing your new favorite pair of black tights, Causing says to think about the sheerness (or denier). ”The spectrum goes from 5 to 100 — the larger the number, the thicker and less sheer the tights get.” Sheer tights can offer a sultry finishing touch to a look, while thicker, more opaque black tights offer more coverage for those who seek it. Also, fuller coverage tights are great for warmth in the colder months. “A little more minimalist? Stick to an easy 50 denier, which should be everyone’s staple,” says Causing.
  • Durability: While one might not associate durability with black tights, advancements in technology and fabrics now allow for tights to handle more wear and tear than ever before. It’s important to consider what level of durability you require in a pair of tights and when or where you’ll be wearing them most. Having a pet is also a big consideration. If you’re a pet owner, we found that Sheertex Classic Rip-Resist tights stand up to claws. If you wear black tights regularly or are concerned about clothing waste, Causing encourages you to look for brands touting durability.
  • Comfort and Fit: You want tights that not only make you look great and are durable but also comfortable with the right amount of stretch. The Wolford Satin Touch Tights get high marks for all of the above. Finding the right fit is key, with personal preference playing a role. Some people prefer the high-rise tights for their smoothing and secure fit, while others may find them constricting and prefer a mid-rise tight. Many of the tights we featured have size guides on the website to help you find the best fit, or you can go into the store to try them on. Our overall top choice, Spanx Tight-End Tights, is a good choice for their size-inclusive range. You should also keep in mind the weight of the tight. A thick tight will feel good in the winter, but you may want to switch to a sheer tight in the summer.
  • Price: Tights run the gamut when it comes to pricing. The tights we tested range from $5 to $75, with a median price of $13. In our testing, we found that sometimes you get what you pay for, with more durable and comfortable tights coming in at a higher price range (usually around $25). However, we found some great budget picks that are easier on the wallet and less of a blow in case they do catch a snag or a run, like the No Nonsense Black Tights and the Hanes Control-Top Blackout Tights.

How We Tested

We tested 28 pairs of black tights ranging from luxury brands to Amazon finds. After all tests were complete, we rated each pair of tights based on a 1 to 5 scale (5 being the best) against the following testing attributes: stretch, opaqueness, durability, and value. Ultimately, the tights with the highest average ratings earned a spot on this PEOPLE Tested list. Here is the specifics on our testing methods:

  • Texture and Feel: We evaluated the texture and feel of the tights by trying them on and noting initial observations on softness and stretch.
  • Stretch: Using clamps, the tights were stretched even further to see just how far these tights could go. We stretched each pair lengthwise from waist to toe and also stretched the width of the waistband. Two separate testers held opposite ends of the tights with clamps and pulled as far as they could. Once they let go, they noted whether the tights were able to make it back to their original shape.
  • Opacity: Next, we moved on to evaluate the opacity of each pair of tights by stretching the tight and holding it up in front of a spotlight, noting how much light was visible through the leg of the tight.
  • Durability: Our durability tests were extensive and thorough with each tester rubbing both soft and hard Velcro across the tights while observing any snags, pulls, or tears. We then moved on to sharp jewelry like rings and claws to see how the tights would fare. The final durability test was dragging a Nimble (a small wearable, finger-blade) across the tights and assessing the damage.
