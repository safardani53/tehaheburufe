PARENTS are racing to their nearest Poundland store, desperate to nab brand new Elf on the Shelf goodies.

So if your little darling, or someone you know, is a big fan of Elf on the Shelf, you’ll need to check this out.

What started out as a cheeky elf, literally sitting on a shelf, has now expanded into a huge range of tasty treats.

There’s a variety of great stocking fillers available, with prices starting at just £1.

Savvy shoppers were stunned after spotting the new range, and many took to social media to alert other shoppers to the bargain buys.

One Poundland fan, Ashleigh Heron, took to Facebook to show off her finds, posting on Extreme Couponing and Bargains UK, a private group with a staggering 2.3 million members.

Showing off the goodies, the mother wrote: “Elf on the Shelf Christmas treats at Poundland.”

Ashleigh spotted milk chocolate lollipops, which are just £1 each and a perfect festive treat that’s sure to put a smile on many children’s faces this Christmas.

She also found giant Scout elf cookies, North pole sherbet straws and candy icicle sticks.

If you’re worried about keeping your kids entertained this Christmas holiday, there’s also a decorate your own chocolate scout elf kit, which includes a milk chocolate elf, red and green icing, and sprinkles.

The kit will cost you just £2 but will make a great stocking filler, or fun activity whilst the kids are off school. 

If that wasn’t enough, there’s also strawberry flavour marshmallow lollipops, for just £1.

Ashleigh shared her post just 18 hours ago, but it has clearly impressed many, as it has quickly amassed 312 likes and 409 comments.

Social media users were open-mouthed at the epic range of treats and were eager to express this in the comments.

One person said: “Need Poundland tomorrow.” 

Another added: “I need to go.”

A third commented: “Definitely getting some of this.” 

Whilst someone else claimed: “Off to Poundland.”

At the same time, many shoppers tagged their friends and loved ones to ask them to nab them these bargain buys.

One shopper tagged a Facebook user and wrote: “Keep an eye out for these.” 

Whilst another asked a family member: “You are going to need to get me all this for Elf's arrival.”
