Ole Gunnar Solskjr (KSO) is a Norwegian professional football manager and former striker. He was a member of the Norwegian clubs Clausenengen and Molde. He joined Manchester United in 1996 for a cost of £1.5 million. He spent his whole professional career with Manchester United, where he appeared in 365 games and scored 126 goals. He won six Premier League titles, two FA Cups, the FA Charity/Community Shield, one UEFA Champions League, and the Intercontinental Cup with Manchester United. He was known as a “super-sub” because of his ability to come off the bench and score late goals. In injury time at the end of the 1999 UEFA Champions League Final, he scored the game-winning goal against Bayern Munich with Manchester United behind 1-0 after 90 minutes, completing The Treble for United. After failing to recuperate from a catastrophic knee injury, he announced his retirement from football in 2007.

He has represented Norway at several age groups on the international stage. He has represented Norway at both the U21 and senior levels. On November 26, 1995, he made his international debut for Norway. He was a member of Norway’s squads that competed in the 1998 FIFA World Cup and the UEFA Euro 2000. He made his final appearance for Norway against Croatia on February 7, 2007. From 1995 to 2007, he made 67 appearances for Norway, scoring 23 goals.
