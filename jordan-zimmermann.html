Jordan Zimmermann | Career and Profession

Before jumping into Zimmermann’s professional career and achievements, let’s look into his impressive early career.

Early Career

During the year 2008, Jordan had a combined 2.89 earned run average and 10–3 record with the Potomac Nationals, a Class A-Advanced Carolina League of the Minor League Baseball team, and Class AA Harrisburg Senators of the Eastern League.

In July, Zimmermann won the Eastern League All-Star team honors. He completed the season leading the organization in strikeouts, earned run average, and wins. MILB.com also named Jordan as the Nationals pitcher of the year.

Professional Career

The Washington Nationals officially drafted Zimmermann during the second round of the 2007 Major League Baseball draft.

Washington Nationals

2009

While representing the Washington Nationals, Baseball America rated Jordan as the Nationals’ best prospect going into the 2009 MLB season.

In 2009, he made it as the fifth-starter during the Nationals’ roster. Although, the Washington Nationals did not want him in the rotation until mid-April.

However, Jordan opened the season with the Syracuse Chiefs, a Minor League Baseball team affiliated to New York Mets as the Triple-A level.

His contract was acquired on April 20th, 2009, and made his major league debut against the Atlanta Braves the same night.

While playing against the New York Mets, Jordan won his second consecutive game as many starts, becoming the first pitcher to win the first two starts at the Nationals/Expos. The first player to set that record was Randy Johnson in 1988.

Moreover, in July, Zimmermann experienced elbow pain, and the Washington Nationals kept him on the disabled list. After rehab, in August 2009, he was again diagnosed with a torn ulnar collateral ligament and received Tommy John’s surgery.

He was expected to miss the games for 18 months. He ended his 2009 rookie season with 3–5 and a 4.63 ERA during 16 starts.

2010

Furthermore, in 2010, Jordan made quick progress. He started in 10 games with limited play in four minor games. He compiled a solid record of 27 hits, 1.59 ERA, six walks, and 31 strikeouts.

On August 26th, the Nationals recalled Zimmermann to make his 2010 debut back in the major leagues. He got a no-decision in an ultimate win by the Nationals while playing against the Cardinals.

He pitched six shutout innings, with only one hit, no walks, a personal best, and nine strikeouts. Moreover, Jordan became the first Washington National Player to get through six innings facing 18 batsmen.

2011

On May 6th, 2011, Jordan pitched an undefiled inning while playing against the Florida Marlins at the Sun Life Stadium. He ended the 2011 season year with a 3.18 ERA.

2012

Jordan hit his first career home run while facing pitcher Carlos Zambrano of the Miami Marlins on May 28th, 2012. He became the third Nationals pitcher who won the NL Pitcher of the Month honors on August 2nd, 2012.

Moreover, in the 2012’s National League Division Series Game- 4, he made an appearance during the 7th inning.

2013

Jordan flung his first career shutout on April 26th, 2013, with a one-hitter playing against the Cincinnati Reds.

It was the second straight one-hitter by the Washington Nationals players during the series, which was the first occurrence since 1917. 

In 2013, Jordan was called by the MLB for the very first time to play at the All-Star Game. However, he chose not to play due to stiffness in his neck.

He finished the 2013 season with a career-high in wins, a 3.25 ERA, and 161 strikeouts in a career-high innings in his 32 starts. His total wins were tied with Adam Wainwright of the National League.

2014

During the final day of the regular season, on September 28th, 2014, Jordan threw the first no-hitter while playing against the Miami Marlins.

He faced a seventh-inning third-strike wild pitch that let Garrett Jones reach first base, separated from him to a perfect game, and a fifth-inning walk to Justin Bour.

His performance was also the first in MLB history where throw a no-hitter on the final day during two consecutive seasons. He ended the season with a record of 14–5 and an ERA of 2.66, with a career-high 182 batters strikeout.

2015

Moreover, Jordan finished the 2015 season with a 13–10 record with an ERA of 3.66, 164 strikeouts, and 1.205 WHIP.

After the season, Zimmermann became one of the 20 players to receive a qualifying offer.

He finished his term with the Washington Nationals starting at 178 games, sporting a record of 70-50, an ERA of 3.32, 1094 pitching innings, eight complete games with four shutouts, and 903 batters strikeouts.

Detroit Tigers

2016

The Detroit Tigers signed a five-year contract worth $110 million with Zimmermann on November 30th, 2015. He won the American League Pitcher of the Month honor for April.

Jordan’s 0.55 ERA was the third-best record for April in the history of Detroit Tigers. The other two Detroit pitchers with more solid ERAs in April were Bernie Boland in 1915 and John Hiller in 1974.

However, the Tigers scheduled Zimmermann to start Tuesday to play against Cleveland, but on July 4th, they placed him on the 5-day disabled list due to a neck strain.

2018

He came back in the rotation on June 16th. Zimmermann’s stats for the 2018 season were 25 starts, compiling a record 7–8 with an ERA of 4.52 and 111 strikeouts.

2019

Furthermore, Jordan opened the season as the starter for the opening day of the Detroit Tigers.
