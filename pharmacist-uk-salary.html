How much does a pharmacist earn? UK salary explained

August 07, 2024
blog

PHARMACISTS play a vital role in society and are often the first resort for medical advice.

With their invaluable knowledge of medications, they are critical for the public's health - but how much do they earn?

What is a pharmacist's salary in the UK?

According to Indeed, a pharmacist earns an average of £47,753 per year.

However, this could be dependent on their work location.

The average wage in Goldalming could earn £58,984 per year, £11,231 more than the UK average.

Alternatively, the average wage in Gloucester is £43,730 per year, £4,023 less than the average.

NHS staff pay is determined by their band or level.

Pharmacists working in a hospital or GP practice usually start on a band 6 pay scale.

As their pay progresses, pharmacists can increase their band level to band 9 as chief pharmacist.

A band 6 pharmacist with less than two years of experience can expect a pay of £35,392 per year.

A band 9 pharmacist with over five years of experience can expect a pay of £114,949 per year.

How can I become a pharmacist?

If you want to become a pharmacist you'll need to complete a five-year programme of academic and practice-based teaching.

This will include studying for a Master's degree in pharmacy for four years at university.

You'll also undertake a foundation training year on a paid work placement for one year.

Once you've completed your placement, you can register with the General Pharmaceutical Council (GPhC).

You'll then be able to legally practice as a pharmacist.

What qualifications do I need to become a pharmacist?

There are no set entry requirements to study pharmacy.

However, you'll generally need three A-Levels or equivalent in chemistry, and a second science such as biology or maths.

University offers usually range from AAB to BBB.

You should check the entry requirements for your chosen course to ensure you get your required grades.

Pharmacy degrees including a foundation year may have lower entry requirements.
