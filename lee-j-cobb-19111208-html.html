Lee J. Cobb is an American actor. He played the role of Willy Loman in director Arthur Miller's original 1949 play Death of a Salesman directed by Elia Kazan. He has also appeared in On the Waterfront, 12 Angry Men, and The Exorcist. On television, Cobb starred in the first four seasons of the Far West series The Virginian. He often plays arrogant, intimidating, and abrasive characters, but he also plays respectable characters like the judge. He was twice nominated for the Best Supporting Actor Academy Award, for The Brothers Karamazov and On the Waterfront.
